The three lines that matter

Comparative efficacy of GLP-1 receptor agonists, exercise, and their combination.

Vandoni M, Gatti A, Rossi V, Giuriato M, Carnevale Pellino V, Zuccotti G, Calcaterra V.
Obesity Reviews, first published 13 July 2026. doi.org/10.1111/obr.70189
Network meta-analysis · 9 randomized trials · 1,009 adults

I read this one so you do not have to dig for the part that changes what you tell a patient. Below are the exact sentences from the paper, highlighted, in the authors' own words. The full open-access paper is one tap below.

1. The combination beat the drug alone

Results, abstract

"The combination of GLP-1 receptor agonists and exercise was associated with the greatest effects on weight (SMD −1.04; 95% CI −1.24 to −0.83), fat mass (SMD −1.01; 95% CI −1.27 to −0.75), and waist-to-hip ratio (SMD −0.55; 95% CI −0.91 to −0.18) compared with placebo."

Section 3.3 and abstract

This is the line for the patient who asks whether they still need to train. Movement is not a nice-to-have next to the prescription, it measurably moves fat mass and waist-to-hip ratio.

2. It also beat the drug alone head to head

Section 3.3, anthropometric outcomes

"Moreover, the combination group obtained greater significant reductions in weight compared with both GLP-1 RAs alone (SMD −0.27, 95% CI −0.47 to −0.07) and exercise alone (SMD −0.53, 95% CI −0.72 to −0.34)."

Section 3.3

Not just better than placebo. Better than the medication by itself, which is the comparison a patient on a GLP-1 actually cares about.

3. The muscle problem, in the authors' words

Discussion, lean body mass

"Reductions in LBM are estimated at 15%–25% with lifestyle interventions, 25%–40% with GLP-1 RAs, and approximately 31% following bariatric surgery... Evidence indicates that resistance training during caloric restriction can preserve 50%–95% of lean mass and support bone density."

Discussion, lean body mass section

This is the one I would put in front of a patient. A quarter to 40% of what they lose can be lean mass. Resistance training is the documented lever against it.

The authors' conclusion

"Combined GLP-1 RA and structured exercise interventions improved weight, insulin sensitivity, and selected lipid parameters more than monotherapies or placebo."
