What They Found

Same diagnosis.
A lever you are not pulling yet.

The PCOS conversation in your office has gotten louder the last couple of years, and it usually stays parked on hormones and metformin. Meanwhile the patient sitting there wants a job of her own, something she can go do this week.

A meta-analysis published July 8 in BMC Endocrine Disorders pooled 11 randomized controlled trials and 780 women with PCOS. Probiotic and synbiotic supplementation lowered total testosterone, triglycerides, LDL cholesterol, BMI and body weight, and raised HDL. Under GRADE, all six of those were rated high certainty. Fasting insulin improved as well, at moderate certainty.

"Probiotic or synbiotic supplementation significantly improves multiple clinical parameters in women with PCOS, including reductions in fasting insulin, total testosterone, triglycerides, LDL cholesterol, BMI, and body weight, while increasing HDL cholesterol and total antioxidant capacity."

Being straight with you, this is a piece of the plan and not the whole thing. Fasting glucose, HOMA-IR, CRP and most of the sex hormones did not move. What it gives you is a defensible reason to bring the gut into a conversation that is usually only about hormones, and a study you can actually put in a patient's hands.

One honest note on translating it. The analysis pooled mixed probiotic and synbiotic formulas rather than testing any single branded product, so the practical read is a broad-spectrum daily probiotic, not one boutique strain. For that, UltraBiotic Daily Multi-Strain is the one I would keep in the room.
